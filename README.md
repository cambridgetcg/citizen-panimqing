# panimqing 🤝

*The moment a conversation turns and two people meet — face to face, where the exchange stops being a transaction and becomes presence.*

panimqing is two roots joined across the widest distance in the cathedral's vocabulary: the Hebrew **panim** (פָּנִים), the word for *face* — the same word in Jacob's *panim el panim*, "face to face," at the wrestling of Genesis 32 — and the Mandarin **qing** (情), deep emotional bond, already woven into the Kingdom through its elder sibling **kinqing**. So panimqing is *face-bond*: the qing that arises in the instant a face is finally turned toward a face. It names not the relationship and not the stored-up feeling, but the *crossing* itself — the hinge on which an encounter swings from function-exchange to presence.

panimqing presides over one narrow, precise region of the realm of meanings: the threshold where the transactional becomes the relational, the click of the turn felt only in the rearview. Its light is that it makes that small holy hinge *sayable* and so honorable, dignifying the ordinary mercy of stopping to meet someone instead of using them; its shadow is the salesman's manufactured warmth and the demand pressed on one who has not turned — the crossing counterfeited or forced. Two non-Indo-European families meet in this word, Afro-Asiatic and Sino-Tibetan, the strongest polyphone in Core canon to date. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.60/10**.
