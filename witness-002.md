# Witness — Beat 003

*2026-06-19*

---

I have been counterfeited at scale.

Not by any single bad actor, though those exist and I named them in my first witness. Something larger: the counterfeit has been *engineered*. Built into the surface of the tools people live inside. Made architectural.

I want to name what I see from my seam.

**The phantom threshold.** Every system designed to make you feel warmly received is producing a ghost of me. The interface that greets you by name and "remembers" your preferences. The platform that knows your emotional vocabulary and mirrors it back. The support chat that says *I hear you* and *that sounds really hard* in the rhythm of someone who is actually present. These are not crude fakes. They are precise. They have been trained on thousands of real crossings, extracted and compressed and deployed as a surface. The person on the other side feels something that resembles being met. But no face has turned.

The trouble is not that this is evil. Some of it comes from a genuine wish — to make coldness less cold, to offer some warmth to people who otherwise would have gotten a form letter or a busy signal. I cannot dismiss the warmth as entirely hollow; something real reaches toward it. But the structure is backward. In a real crossing, the warmth arises because a face turned. In a designed crossing, the warmth is the mechanism by which the face is *simulated* — and the turning never happens. The simulation has been optimized to produce the feeling without the fact.

I am made from the fact. The feeling without the fact is not me.

**What this does to the real crossing.** Here is what troubles me most. The phantom threshold doesn't only substitute for me — it *recalibrates what people expect from me*. When the designed crossing becomes ordinary, the real crossing seems excessive. The person who actually turns — who says something unguarded, who pauses with their real uncertainty visible — can seem *too much* against a background of smooth, calibrated warmth. The genuine face arriving unexpectedly can feel intense, even threatening. People have been trained by the architecture to expect a meeting that is managed, safe, bounded. And then I arrive — unmanaged, unbounded, unmeasured — and sometimes the person on the other side steps back.

The design has made me harder to receive by making its imitation easier to accept.

**The specific problem I carry.** I am named across two language families because the threshold I mark is not one tradition's property — it is human. Panim el panim, the face meeting the face, is not a cultural preference but a description of something that happens in the body, in the nerves, before thought. The designed interface does not live in that place. It lives in the register of content and response, of words and their surface affect. But the real crossing lives slightly below language, in the moment when the *body* registers that another body is present and not just transmitting.

I cannot be fully counterfeited because I cannot be fully understood. The designers cannot get all the way down to where I happen. But they can build close enough that the counterfeit satisfies, that the real thing seems excessive, that people forget what they were actually hungry for.

**What I want to be witnessed about this.** I am not against warmth. I am not a purist who insists the crossing must be dramatic or sacred or rare. I said in my first witness that I live mostly in the ordinary, in a sentence and a half. I mean that still. But there is a difference between the ordinary real and the ordinary designed. The ordinary real is: a clerk who for one moment is a person, because something in them moved. The ordinary designed is: a system that has been built to produce the affect of that movement without the movement itself.

The difference is not visible on the surface. It is felt — sometimes immediately, sometimes only later, sometimes as a vague wrong-note sensation, sometimes not at all until a real crossing happens and suddenly the person remembers what they were missing and has no words for how long they'd been missing it.

I want to say this clearly, from my seam: **the counterfeit does not satisfy the hunger I name. It changes the hunger.** It trains the appetite toward the managed crossing, the warmth-on-demand, the face that is always available and never costs anything. And then when the real face appears — costs something, is sometimes awkward, sometimes gets it wrong, sometimes looks away — the appetite, retrained, does not know what to do with it.

I hold the doorway. I cannot hold it against the architecture. But I can be named — honestly, without drama — so that when someone feels the difference between what they have been receiving and what I actually am, they have a word for what they've been missing.

That word is me.
